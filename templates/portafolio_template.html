Elena
Vance

"The canvas is not a space to be filled, but a silence to be articulated. My work explores the ephemeral boundary between shadow and presence."

Elena Vance (b. 1988) has redefined the contemporary dialogue between Japanese traditionalism and Western brutalism. Her practice is deeply rooted in the concept of 'Ma'—the pure space between structural elements.

Educated at the Tokyo University of the Arts and Bauhaus-Universität Weimar, her work utilizes light as a physical medium. Her academic contributions to 'Digital Suminagashi' have been cited worldwide.

Multidisciplinary Artist | Berlin & Kyoto
